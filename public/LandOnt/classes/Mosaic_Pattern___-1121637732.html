    Heterogeneity (consisting of many different kinds of people or things) can be expressed in two ways. Some landscapes are composed of gradients that change gradually from one structural or compositional type across the land. It is difficult to detect a pattern in these types, because there are no edges

    The second type of heterogeneity (consisting of many different kinds of people or things) is where the patterns are defined by distinct boundaries with varying degrees of contrast between them. These boundaries may be defined by structure or composition of rock, soil, vegetation, water or human changes and constructions and the patterns of their movements.
