    Fractal geometry. Benoit Mandelbrot's concept grew from
    the realization that scientific method and euclidean geometry,
    in their effort to classify and simplify, did not adequately
    represent the scale independence or complexity of
    natural forms and systems. In fractal geometry, the level of
    complexity increases as the scale increases, so that apparent
    complexity remains consistent. Occurring in nature,
    fractals express a behavior or form irrespective of scale.
    Fractals are part of the new science of chaos."
