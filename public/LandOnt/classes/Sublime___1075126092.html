    74/75
    The similarities between beauty and sublime are:
    • Both are pleasing.
    • Both involve judgment based solely on reflection, not on logic or common sense.
    • Both provide pleasure from the presentation of an immediate insight
    • Both demonstrate an accord (a formal agreement between two organizations, countries, etc) between our imagination and conceptual faculties (any of the physical or mental abilities that a person is born with).
    • Both exist as unique judgments.
    • Both are universally valid for all observers.

    The differences are:
    • completeness and unity of the form  beauty/ formlessness, complexity and incomprehensibility  sublime
    • In both we are presented with indeterminate concepts, but that for beauty is one of understanding, whilst for the sublime it is of reason.
    • Beauty is more concerned with quality, the sublime with quantity.
    • In beauty our emotions tend to be directed to the furtherance (the process of helping sth to develop or to be successful) of life whereas with the sublime, after the initial sense of pleasure, our energies are checked as a more powerful emotion surges through us, one that is intensely sensuous (giving pleasure to your senses) and not wanting to be given delight by the scene.
